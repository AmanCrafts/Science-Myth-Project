"Incognito Mode Makes You Invisible Online"

Explanation of the Myth:

Many users believe that when they use Incognito Mode (or Private Browsing) in their web browser, they are completely anonymous and invisible on the internet. They think that it hides their browsing history from everyone, including websites, their internet service provider (ISP), and other entities that may track their online activity.

Debunking the Myth:

  • Incognito Mode Only Hides Local Data:

    When you use Incognito or Private Mode, it prevents the browser from saving certain information, such as your browsing history, cookies, and temporary files. However, it does not hide your activity from websites you visit, your ISP, or anyone monitoring the network (like your employer or the government). Websites can still track your activity using your IP address and browser fingerprinting, which are not affected by Incognito Mode.

  • ISP and Network Monitoring:

    Your Internet Service Provider (ISP) can still see the websites you visit, regardless of whether you’re using Incognito Mode. This is because the traffic is still being routed through their servers. In fact, your ISP can still log your activity and can track you based on your IP address.

  • Online Tracking by Websites:

    Many websites and advertisers use cookies and tracking pixels to monitor your online behavior, even if you’re using Incognito Mode. These methods don’t depend on cookies stored in your browser but use other techniques like browser fingerprinting, which can uniquely identify and track your device.

  • No Protection from Malware or Phishing:

    Incognito Mode does not protect you from malware, phishing, or other forms of cyberattack. It doesn’t encrypt your traffic or block harmful sites. For better protection, you would need a VPN to hide your IP address and encrypt your browsing data, or use browser extensions that block trackers and malicious websites.

  • Misleading Perceptions:

    Many users think Incognito Mode prevents all kinds of tracking, but it only hides local data stored on the device. Your actions are still visible to websites, advertisers, and your ISP. If you're serious about privacy, it's essential to use additional tools like a VPN, Tor Browser, or privacy-focused search engines (e.g., DuckDuckGo).

Conclusion:

Incognito Mode does not make you invisible on the internet. It only prevents your browser from storing browsing history and other local data. If you want full online anonymity and privacy, you need to use other tools like a VPN (to hide your IP address and encrypt your traffic) and Tor (for anonymous browsing), as well as being mindful of the ways websites and trackers can still identify and follow your online activities.
